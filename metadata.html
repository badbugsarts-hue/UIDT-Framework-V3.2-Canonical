Abstract

This manuscript presents UIDT version 3.4, a conservative revision introducing a fundamental scalar field S(x) representing vacuum information density. The framework proposes a mechanism for generating a Yang-Mills mass gap through non-minimal coupling (κ/Λ)S·Tr(FμνFμν) and vacuum condensate dynamics.

The canonical parameters emerge from a self-consistent three-equation system yielding:

  • Δ = 1.710 ± 0.015 GeV (mass gap)
  • κ = 0.500 ± 0.008 (coupling constant)
  • λS = 0.417 ± 0.007 (self-coupling)
  • γ ≈ 16.339 (universal invariant)

These values exhibit numerical self-consistency with residuals of order 10-14 and show statistical agreement with lattice QCD determinations (z-score ≈ 0.4).

Limitations

Acknowledged Limitations

  • Electron Mass Discrepancy: 23% deviation (predicted: 0.392 MeV, observed: 0.511 MeV)
  • λUIDT Ambiguity: 29% discrepancy between versions (0.854 nm vs 0.66 nm)
  • S₈ Tension: 6σ discrepancy with ACT DR6 measurements
  • No UV Completion: Effective field theory only, not a complete quantum gravity theory
  • Lagrangian Non-Uniqueness: Alternative coupling structures could yield similar phenomenology
