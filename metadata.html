Abstract

This manuscript presents UIDT version 3.5, a consolidated theoretical framework introducing a fundamental scalar field S(x) representing vacuum information density. The framework proposes a mechanism for generating a Yang-Mills mass gap through non-minimal coupling (κ/Λ)S·Tr(FμνFμν) and vacuum condensate dynamics.

The canonical parameters emerge from a self-consistent three-equation system yielding:

  • Δ = 1.710 ± 0.015 GeV (mass gap)
  • κ = 0.500 ± 0.008 (coupling constant)
  • λS = 0.417 ± 0.007 (self-coupling)
  • γ ≈ 16.339 (universal invariant)

In the cosmological sector, the framework adopts a DESI-optimized scale of λUIDT = 0.66 nm. This choice offers a potential resolution to current tensions, predicting H₀ = 70.4 ± 0.16 km/s/Mpc (consistent with JWST CCHP) and S₈ = 0.757 ± 0.002 (consistent with ACT DR6).
